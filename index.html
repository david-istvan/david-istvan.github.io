Research interest
My research interests include

  • design of domain-specific languages,
  • complex event processing.

You can find further information on this page about the projects I am involved in, the list of my publications and talks and you can also take a look at my CV.

Research

Inconsistency management in collaborative modeling settings

I am currently a member of the MSDL (Modelling, Simulation and Design Lab), working on my PhD under the supervision of Prof. Hans Vangheluwe and within the MBSE4Mechatronics project. My research focuses on inconsistency management in collaborative, multi-model system development.

Complex event processing (CEP) and its applications

VIATRA (VIsual Automated model TRAnsformations) is an event-driven and reactive model transformation platform. My primary contribution covers the complex event processing (CEP) module and the incremental API of the core transformation framework. This work is carried out in collaboration with the Fault Tolerant Systems Research Group of the Budapest University of Technology and Economics.
